To accomplish this assignment, I used lots of col- classes. The overarching container is a container-lg, since I wanted the container to take up a lot of screen real estate. I looked up how to do margins with Bootstrap, and then applied a Y margin (my) to large screens of the container, so it's not touching against the top of the browser on desktop. I also added a small one to meal paragraphs so they're not touching the images.

When it comes to the divs inside the rows, I defined col-sm (small breakpoint) for small screens, col-md (medium breakpoint) for medium-sized screens, and col-lg (large breakpoint) for larger and beyond screens. That way the divs don't horizontally overflow on smaller devices. I made sure each number I used added up to 12--the total number of cols in a Bootstrap row. I then looked up how to do paddings in Bootstrap, and applied a padding to each div inside of a row.
